How to use the red

Signal Red is reserved for the "RE" wordmark, CTAs, the tagline, key stats, and a single accent rule per layout. If everything is red, nothing is. Aim for roughly 5% coverage on any given surface.

Backgrounds

Alternate between Soft White and Mist for a calm reading rhythm. Deep Navy owns the hero, footer, and major section breaks. Steel Blue has been deprecated in favor of Twilight and Mist — a tighter, more intentional pair.

The problem

For patients recovering from spinal cord injury, stroke, and amputation, rehab is often the moment play disappears. Standard equipment doesn't fit. Peer connection thins. Motivation dips at the exact point it's needed most.

Our approach

We embed adaptive gaming stations — with clinician-designed protocols — directly into inpatient and outpatient rehab. Occupational and physical therapists use play as a clinical modality, measured and documented.

What's next

Expanding to 25 partner hospitals by end of 2027, publishing open protocols for the field, and growing our clinician fellowship to 60 OTs and PTs trained in adaptive gaming practice.

08 · Usage Rules

What to do, what never to do.

Keep the lockup compact and square-safe. Protect clear space. Never recolor the mark outside the palette. Never stretch, skew, outline, or drop-shadow the logo.

Do · clear space

Maintain clear space of at least 1× the mascot's height around the entire lockup.

Do · mono on photo
RESPAWN
Foundation

On imagery, use the single-color white lockup placed over sufficiently dark, uncluttered areas.

Don't · recolor
RESPAWN
Foundation

Never swap the accent out for off-brand hues. The red is the red; it signals the RE.

Don't · stretch or skew
RESPAWN
Foundation

The mark and wordmark proportions are fixed. Scale uniformly. Never distort.

Don't · outline or shadow
RESPAWN
Foundation

No glows, no strokes, no drop shadows on the logo. Let it sit flat.

Don't · crowd the mark
RESPAWN
Foundation

Never place frames, containers, or competing graphics inside the clear-space zone.
